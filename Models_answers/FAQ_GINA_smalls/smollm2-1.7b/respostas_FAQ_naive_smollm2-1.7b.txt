Inflammation of the airways is a major underlying cause of asthma symptoms. When the airways in the lungs become inflamed, they become swollen, narrowed, and more sensitive to various triggers. This can lead to a range of symptoms, such as coughing, wheezing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath.

Here are some ways in which inflammation affects asthma:

1. **Narrowing of the airways**: The inflamed airways become swollen and narrowed, making it harder for air to flow in and out of the lungs. This can lead to a reduction in airflow and cause symptoms like coughing, wheezing, and chest tightness.

2. **Increased mucus production**: In response to the inflammation, the airways produce more mucus, which can clog the airways and make it harder to breathe. This can lead to a persistent cough and make it harder for you to expel mucus from your lungs.

3. **Increased responsiveness to triggers**: The inflamed airways are more sensitive to various triggers, such as allergens, pollutants, and irritants. When you come into contact with these triggers, your airways can become inflamed and irritated, leading to more severe symptoms.

4. **Increased airway resistance**: Inflammation in the airways can make the airways more resistant to the flow of air, which can lead to increased airway resistance. This can make it harder for your lungs to expand and fill with air, leading to shortness of breath.

5. **Hyperresponsiveness**: The inflamed airways can become highly responsive to even small amounts of inhaled allergens or irritants, leading to an exaggerated allergic response and worsening of symptoms.

Overall, inflammation of the airways is a key underlying cause of asthma symptoms, and controlling inflammation is a critical part of managing the condition. This is why medications such as corticosteroids and leukotriene modifiers are often used to reduce inflammation and alleviate asthma symptoms.
