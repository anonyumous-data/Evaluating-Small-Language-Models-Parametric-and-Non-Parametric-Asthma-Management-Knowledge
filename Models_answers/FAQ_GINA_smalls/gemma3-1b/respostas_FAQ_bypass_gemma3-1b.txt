* **What it is:** This technique is specifically helpful for breath-hold asthma.
* **How to do it:**
    * **Inhale:** Breathe in for 4 counts.
    * **Hold:** Hold your breath for 7 counts.
    * **Exhale:** Slowly exhale for 8 counts.
    * **Repeat:** Do 4-5 rounds of this.
* **Why it helps:** It helps to calm your nervous system and slows down your breathing rate.

* **What it is:** A rhythmic breathing pattern that focuses on slow, deep breaths.
* **How to do it:**
    * **Inhale:** Inhale for 4 counts.
    * **Hold:** Hold for 4 counts.
    * **Exhale:** Exhale for 4 counts.
    * **Hold:** Hold for 4 counts.
    * **Repeat:** Do 5-10 rounds.
* **Why it helps:** The rhythm can be calming and help regulate your breathing.


**4.  Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR):**

* **What it is:**  This is a technique to reduce muscle tension, which can worsen asthma symptoms.
* **How to do it:**
    * **Find a comfortable position:** Lie down or sit comfortably.
    * **Tense and Release:**  Starting with your toes, tense the muscles in that area for 5-10 seconds.  Then, completely release the tension (no longer holding) for 10-20 seconds.  Repeat this process, working your way up your body (feet, calves, thighs, etc.).
* **Why it helps:**  Tense muscles can trigger asthma symptoms.

**5.  Counting:**

* **What it is:**  Focusing on counting can interrupt the breathing pattern and help regain control.
* **How to do it:**  Count backward from 100 to 1.  When you reach 1, start counting again.

* **Short-Acting Inhalers (SAs):** These are used for quick relief of asthma symptoms, like asthma attacks. They’re usually prescribed *in addition* to ICS for long-term prevention.
* **Bronchodilators:** These medications relax the muscles around the airways, opening them up and helping to relieve symptoms like wheezing and shortness of breath. They're helpful for triggering symptoms.

    * **Asthma Action Plan:**  A written plan that outlines how to manage symptoms, when to use rescue medications, and when to seek medical attention.
* **Not All Medications Work for Everyone:**  The effectiveness of medication can vary from person to person.
